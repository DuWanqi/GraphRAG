A great Marxist, proletarian revolutionary, politician, military strategist, and diplomat, he was a long-tested communist fighter and a great internationalist who significantly contributed to world peace and development.

Deng Xiaoping was the core of the Party's second-generation central leadership collective and the chief architect of China's socialist reform and opening up and modernization drive. He pioneered the path of socialism with Chinese characteristics and was the main founder of Deng Xiaoping Theory, which was enshrined in the Party Constitution as a guiding ideology alongside Marxism-Leninism and Mao Zedong Thought. His theories and important talks, compiled in 《邓小平文选》, provided theoretical guidance for reform and opening up, emphasizing the need to liberate thought and seek truth from facts.

His early life included work-study programs in Europe before dedicating himself to the revolutionary struggle led by the Communist Party. He held important leadership positions in the Party and the army during the Agrarian Revolution, Anti-Japanese War, and Liberation War, and was a commander, along with Liu Bocheng, of the Liu Deng Army.

Deng Xiaoping experienced multiple political comebacks, with his third affirmed on July 16, 1977, when he was restored to key positions within the Chinese Communist Party and the government. With Mao Zedong's support, he began to comprehensively manage the daily work of the CPC Central Committee and the State Council, rectifying chaotic situations. At the Theoretical Work Symposium of the Party, he first proposed the "Four Cardinal Principles."

He delivered a key speech titled "Emancipate the Mind, Seek Truth from Facts, and Unite as One to Look Forward" before the Third Plenary Session of the 11th CPC Central Committee. On August 18, 1980, he proposed reforms to the party and state leadership system, advocating for the abolition of lifelong tenure for cadres.

Deng Xiaoping's "reform and opening up" policies and "one country, two systems" changed China and influenced the world, earning him "Person of the Year" by Time Magazine in 1978 and 1985.      "经济特区," or Economic Special Zones, are distinct regions within China that implement a unique set of preferential policies and measures, sharing similarities with Economic and Technological Development Zones. Initially conceived as "Export Special Zones" by Deng Xiaoping in April 1979, the concept was renamed in March 1980 and first implemented in Shenzhen.

These zones function as a significant type of world free port area, leveraging various incentives to foster economic and technological advancement within China. Key preferential measures include tariff reductions, the creation of a favorable investment environment, and the active encouragement of foreign investment. The primary objectives are to introduce advanced technology and scientific management practices from abroad, thereby stimulating the host country's economic and technological development.

Economic Special Zones operate under special economic policies, flexible economic measures, and unique economic management systems, with an overarching goal of export-oriented economic development. Their emergence in the late 1970s and early 1980s marked a crucial phase in China's reform and opening-up policy, experiencing substantial growth throughout the 1990s.

There are currently seven Economic Special Zones in mainland China. The first four were established in July 1979 in Shenzhen, Zhuhai, and Shantou (all in Guangdong Province), and Xiamen (Fujian Province), and were officially renamed Economic Special Zones in May 1980. The Hainan Economic Special Zone was subsequently established in April 1988. Following 1992, the model's success led to its expansion to national-level new areas, such as Shanghai Pudong, signifying a new wave of reforms in China.
